Its role is to collect, conserve and restore collections related to Oceania, and more particularly to Polynesia, and to present them to the public. It is in charge of the development, the study and the diffusion of the Polynesian heritage. It has acquired a role of expertise in the preservation of cultural and movable property.
All collections combined, the museum houses approximately 30,000 objects:
-13,000 are parts of the Herbarium of French Polynesia6;
-11,500 are ethnographic objects;
-5,500 are art objects.
The museum grounds are home to the Atea Ethnobotanical Garden and the "Tipaerui Twins" petroglyph that was found on the west bank of the Tipaerui River.
